From Law to Computer Science

February 4, 2023 2-minute read

As someone who has studied both law and computer science, I have come to realize that my true passion lies in the latter field. While a law degree can certainly be valuable in terms of developing people skills, such as the ability to interpret and apply rules and concepts, I have found that my interests lie more in exploring the universe through the lens of computer science and programming.

It’s like playing a game of Grand Theft Auto or a massively multiplayer online role-playing game. The experience of completing missions and minigames to increase your rank is certainly fun, but I find it even more enjoyable to explore the open world and discover hidden gems.

Teach You how to Think vs the Dullness of Law Link to heading

One of the most powerful conceptual frameworks in computer science is the use of graphs, which can be used to model everything from protein structures to social networks and even the human body. Additionally, concepts such as recursion and divide and conquer are essential for developing efficient and effective solutions to complex problems.

In contrast, I have found that studying law can sometimes make me feel like I am dumber, as the subject matter can be quite specific and difficult to grasp. Furthermore, while the ability to manipulate people and navigate the hierarchy of a society can be a valuable skill to have, I am more interested in using my skills to build systems that are used by a large number of people.

The Excitement of Applied Problem Solving Link to heading

In my part-time job, I used to spend a lot of time doing report summaries for my boss by copying and pasting information from several websites. However, with web scraping and with today’s LLM capabilities, I realized that this task could be completely automatable.

Why I Chose Computer Science as My Career Link to heading

In conclusion, while a law degree can provide valuable people skills, I have found that my passions lie in the exciting world of computer science. By utilizing my skills in programming and AI, hopefully I can build systems that are used by many people and contribute to solving complex problems in our world.
